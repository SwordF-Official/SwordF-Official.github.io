Khan Academy

> Click here to attempt to visit my old KA Subpage <
> Click here to visit my new KA Subpage <

Back in my sophomore year, I used to get really bored in class, and while we had our own computers, they were extremly managed by the "school" and blocked practically every site except trusted sites like Khan Academy, which happen that you can code your own projects and share them.

So, I made an account with my school email, and since I didn't want affiliated with the "school" by any means, I decided to do a different name completly. And since I was always bored to death in class every day, I decided to call the account The Bored Guy.

And so, for the next 2-3 years I've made games in their html and (slightly tweeked) p5.js enviroments, because I simply had nothing else to really do, as in that time-frame, I never actually owned an actul computer at home.

And now that I obviously do own one now, I wasn't so sure why to keep doing stuff there. Zooming out and seeing the whole picture, I realized that it was time to try something new and different.

Fast forward a year, and found out that the account would most likely be deleted when I finally got done with school, so I archived the major projects over there, and made an account that wouldn't get deleted.
